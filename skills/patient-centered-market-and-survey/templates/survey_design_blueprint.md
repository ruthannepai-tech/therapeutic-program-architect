# {Condition} Patient-Community Survey — Design Blueprint
*All items are illustrative drafts for patient-advisor and IRB revision — not a final instrument. Nothing here is medical advice.*

## Guiding principles
1. **Nothing about us without us** — patients/caregivers co-design, not just respond.
2. **Anchor to lessons already paid for** — design items that detect the failure modes seen in adjacent programs (safety-risk tolerance, schedule/route burden) *before* they are built in.
3. **Measure what patients feel, with tools patients helped build** — anchor to validated instruments where they exist; build new items transparently where they don't.
4. **Companion to, not replacement for, a formal PFDD meeting.**

## Research questions
1. Current burden of the condition and its management, as patients rank it.
2. Patient understanding of the proposed modality; framing needed for informed response.
3. Efficacy profile worth pursuing; minimum acceptable benefit.
4. Acceptable routes, schedules, administration burden — by severity and life stage.
5. Risk tolerance (esp. serious/immune-mediated events) vs. benefit trade-off.
6. How prior-treatment history shapes fatigue, trust, willingness to enroll.
7. Information/consent/trust-building needed before participation.
8. Companion-diagnostic / screening acceptability and equity barriers.
9. Desired ongoing involvement in development.

## Sampling & strata
- Age band (with caregiver-assisted / adolescent-assent instruments for pediatric-onset disease).
- Age at diagnosis (pediatric- vs. adult-onset).
- Disease-control status (active vs. remission).
- Current management (each modality + combinations).
- Sociodemographic diversity with **deliberate oversampling** of underrepresented groups.
- Target n: state as descriptive/exploratory (e.g. n=400 adults + n=150 caregiver-proxy), **not** hypothesis-testing-powered.

## Recruitment (trusted channels first)
- Primary patient advocacy organization(s) — newsletter, portal, registry (if consented for research contact).
- Family-focused foundation(s) — for pediatric/caregiver reach.
- Clinical research consortium — clinically-verified diagnosis subgroup + refractory patients.
- Snowball/social with plain-language explainer.
- Modest, IRB-reviewed, non-coercive incentive.

## Domains & sample items
**D1. Disease & treatment burden** — frequency Likerts, ranked-burden item, procedure count, open-text "what does it cost you day to day."
**D2. Understanding of the modality** — prior awareness, post-explainer clarity (1–5), open questions.
**D3. Efficacy expectations** — symptom vs. tissue outcome, single most-important outcome, minimum meaningful improvement (% bands).
**D4. Route & schedule tolerance** — preferred route, acceptable visits/yr, induction-schedule effect.
**D5. Risk tolerance** — concern about mechanism-linked risk, highest acceptable serious-event risk, monitoring-setting preference, open-text "what would make you feel it was tested safely."
**D6. Prior-treatment history & fatigue** — treatments tried checklist, count, burnout (1–5), fatigue-driven discontinuation.
**D7. Trust, consent, information needs** — trust in safety testing (1–5), required pre-consent information checklist, effect of patient co-design on trust.
**D8. Companion-diagnostic acceptability** — willingness for blood/genetic test, concerns, cost/travel barrier.
**D9. Involvement & demographics** — interest in advisory roles, forms of involvement, demographics (age, age at dx, sex, race/ethnicity, region, insurance, income [optional], self- vs. caregiver-report).

## Validated-instrument anchors
- Burden/symptom domain → disease-specific FDA-recognized COA(s).
- Efficacy domain → cross-reference the trial symptom-activity index so patient-defined improvement benchmarks to trial endpoints.
- Fatigue/QoL → PROMIS short forms (Fatigue, Global Health, Anxiety).
- Flag domains with **no** validated instrument — the survey's novel contribution and future psychometric-validation candidates.

## Analysis plan
- **Quantitative:** descriptives; segment comparisons (chi-square/Fisher for categorical, Kruskal–Wallis for ordinal Likert); composite burden/risk scores; latent-class/cluster analysis for patient segments if n allows.
- **Qualitative:** inductive thematic coding by ≥2 coders incl. a patient co-analyst; reconciled codebook; retained de-identified quotes for a PFDD briefing.
- **Segmentation reporting:** by pediatric/caregiver vs. adult and by severity — never a single averaged patient.

## Ethics & plain language
6th–8th-grade reading level; health-literacy checklist; cognitive-interview pilot (5–10) before fielding; layered consent (summary + expandable); honest modality explainer (mechanism + real risks, no alarm/false reassurance); translation for the recruitment population; screen-reader-compatible. **IRB approval before any data collection.** Respondents directed to their own clinicians for medical decisions.

## Patient-engagement model (standing, not one-off)
Standing patient advisory board reviews/approves every instrument and reconvenes at each milestone; ≥2 rounds of co-design + cognitive pilot before fielding.
